Animal Vision Explorer

This project uses your camera to simulate how different animals and human colour-vision conditions might interpret the same scene.

What this does

This interactive website shows a live camera view and applies different visual models. Some are based on real biological ideas, while others are conceptual educational visualisations.

Important note

Phones and laptops only capture standard visible-light RGB data. That means UV, thermal, and polarisation modes here are approximations, not direct sensing.

Before you begin
  1. Make sure to turn off all other applications that are using the camera.
  2. Use a browser that supports camera access.
  3. When your browser asks for permission, click Allow for the camera.
  4. If the camera does not appear, reload the page and allow permission.
  5. For best results, point the camera at colourful, bright, or reflective objects.
How to use it
  • Choose a mode from the dropdown.
  • Use Compare to see normal human view beside the selected mode.
  • Move the Split slider to compare both views directly.
  • Use Learn or Colour Guide for explanations.
Best things to test
  • Colourful books, clothes, packaging, or posters
  • Plants, flowers, and printed images
  • Shiny metal, phone screens, or reflective plastic
  • Your hand or face for conceptual contrast modes
If nothing happens after clicking start, check the camera permission icon in your browser address bar.
